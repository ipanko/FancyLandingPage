Description

Periodontitis is an important health public problem which affects millions of people worldwide and it is very frequent oral health problem in daily clinical practice. Although the periodontal disease classification proposed by the American Academy of Periodontology (AAP, 1999) has been widely used for 17 years, scientific evidence was not able to show substantial differences between the main clinical categories of the disease: chronic and aggressive periodontitis. Thus, the 2017 World Workshop on the Classification of Periodontal and Peri-Implant Diseases and Conditions, co-presented by the American Academy of Periodontology (AAP) and the European Federation of Periodontology (EFP) redesigned the disease classification considering the newly scientific evidence in terms of etiology, epidemiology and clinical signs of disease. Moreover, a new classification system for endo-periodontal lesions was also proposed. The knowledge of the new classification is essential for clinicians, researchers, students and professors as it allows the design of appropriate treatment plans based on the best and updated scientific evidence. This online course of 5 hours will provide the attendee with the opportunity to understand, discuss and clarify all doubts about the new classification scheme for periodontitis and endo-periodontal diseases. A new proposal of a clinical end-point for periodontal treatment based on the treat-to-target approach published by the two speakers will also be presented (Feres et al. JIAP 2020).
